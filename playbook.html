01The two-number system

The one thing to understand: the opener and the ceiling come from different places. The opener anchors off their ask. The ceiling is set by resale value. The opener always sits under the ceiling.

OPEN — the handshake
First offer. 15–20% under their asking price, always with a reason (flaw > cash-today > comp).
a tactic, not the economics
WALK — the wall
Max I'll ever pay. Set off resale value R, never their ask. Frozen the moment I open — a seller can only talk me down.
the economics — the hard line

06The bright lines

💰
Open at — 15–20% under their ask, with a reason. Never above walk.
🧱
Never pay above walk — 0.60×R local flip · 0.33×R shipped · 0.85×R personal. Frozen at open, only moves down.
✅
Commit to buy when agreed ≤ walk AND (flip: net ≥ $100 & ≥ 40% margin · personal: ≥ 15% under comp).
🏷️
Resell at the eBay-sold median R (or R×0.95 for a fast local sale).
🛑
Walk when best price is >10% over walk, OR fake / wrong-model / scam, OR net < $100 / margin < 40%.
🔔
Pull you in at: buy-ready · counter just-over-walk · condition/auth flag · logistics. Money always stops for you.
One sentence: Open ~15% under their ask to anchor, never pay more than 60% of what it resells for, buy the moment we land at-or-under that ceiling with ≥$100/40% left, resell at the eBay-sold price, and walk the second the price crosses the ceiling by 10% or anything smells fake.
